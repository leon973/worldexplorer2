About Spain

Spain, located in the Iberian Peninsula in southwestern Europe, is renowned for its rich history, diverse cultural heritage, and world-famous cuisine. From the bustling streets of Madrid to the stunning beaches of the Costa del Sol, and from the historic vineyards of La Rioja to the snowy peaks of the Sierra Nevada, Spain offers an incredible diversity of landscapes and experiences.

As one of the world's most visited countries, Spain boasts iconic landmarks like the Sagrada Familia, the Alhambra, and the Plaza Mayor. Its regions each have distinct identities, languages, and traditions, making every visit unique.

Spanish culture has profoundly influenced art, architecture, gastronomy, and music. The country is home to 49 UNESCO World Heritage Sites and produces some of the world's finest wines and olive oils.

Essential Spanish Phrases

Planning to visit Spain? Here are some useful Spanish phrases to help you communicate during your trip:

Hello / Good day

Spanish: Hola / Buenos días

Pronunciation: oh-la / bwen-os dee-as

Please

Spanish: Por favor

Pronunciation: por fah-vor

Thank you

Spanish: Gracias

Pronunciation: grah-see-as

Where is...?

Spanish: ¿Dónde está...?

Pronunciation: don-deh es-tah

How much does it cost?

Spanish: ¿Cuánto cuesta?

Pronunciation: kwan-to kwes-ta

I don't understand

Spanish: No entiendo

Pronunciation: no en-tee-en-do

Famous Spanish Festivals

Spain hosts numerous vibrant festivals throughout the year. Here are some you shouldn't miss:

March

Las Fallas

A spectacular celebration in Valencia featuring huge satirical sculptures that are eventually set aflame in an impressive night of fireworks and bonfires.

July

Running of the Bulls

Part of the San Fermín festival in Pamplona, where participants run in front of bulls through the streets of the old quarter.

August

La Tomatina

The world's biggest food fight in Buñol, where thousands of participants throw tomatoes at each other in a chaotic and fun festival.

April

Feria de Abril

Seville's colorful spring fair featuring flamenco dancing, horse parades, traditional costumes, and plenty of food and drink.

Spain Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

Spring (April to June) and fall (September to October) offer pleasant weather and fewer crowds. Summer is hot, especially in southern Spain, while winter is mild on the coast but can be cold inland. The northern coast stays green year-round.

Dining Etiquette

Spanish meals follow a later schedule than many countries. Lunch is typically from 2-4pm and dinner from 9-11pm. Tapas are often shared among the group. Spaniards often stand at the bar for casual meals, and tipping is appreciated but not expected to be large.

Transportation

Spain has an excellent high-speed train (AVE) network connecting major cities. In Madrid and Barcelona, the Metro systems are efficient and easy to use. Buses connect smaller towns, and car rentals are good for exploring rural areas and villages.

Cultural Sites

Book tickets online in advance for popular attractions like the Sagrada Familia and Alhambra. Many museums offer free entry during certain hours or days. Dress modestly when visiting churches and cathedrals (covered shoulders and knees).
